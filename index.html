The Divine Path of Harinam Chanting

A Comprehensive Guide to Uniting with Radha and Krishna through Sacred Sound

üíï

The Supremacy of Harinam in Kaliyuga

In the present age, known as Kaliyuga, the spiritual state of the soul (*jeevatma*) is described as piteous: "Slackened mind, intellect and thought processes. Consistently surrounded by problems. Adhi (natural disasters), Vyadhi (illness) and Upadhi (social propriety that come with name and fame) eating away one's mental peace. Sunk in bad association. Easily influenced by Maya. Constantly harassed by material desires." All these describe the state of the jeevatma in this Kaliyuga. If someone is still able to persevere in the bhakti sadhana of Radha Krishna then it is only due to mercy of Guru and rise of samskaras of previous lives. Harinaam is the best and the primary way of bhakti sadhana, all other forms of bhakti are secondary to Harinaam.

"Harer naam harer naam harer naamaiva kevalam,
kalau nasteyiva nasteyiva nasteyiva gatiranyatha."

Meaning: Harinam is the only, only and only way to acquire bhakti. In kaliyuga, there is no, no, just no other way. (The purpose of saying thrice is to emphasize on its importance). Even without any sacrifice or abstinence from pleasures, Harinaam is able to pull the mind from material pleasures and direct one's inclination towards bhakti of Radha Krishna.

Which Name to Chant?

God has innumerable names. They have been described in various Puranas and Smritis. Krishna, Mukunda, Murari, Govind, Ram, Narayan and so on. The *Shrimad Bhagavatam* provides guidance on which name to chant:

"Etam vrata sapriyanaamkirtya yathanurago dhritchittadurjayih,
hasati atha rodati ro'oti gayati unmaadvat nrityati lokbahyah."

Meaning: "'Sapriyanaam' means the name which is dear to you. The name of God which you like, are passionate to chant, and which attracts your heart, should be chanted repeatedly. (It can be different for different people)". By chanting the name repeatedly, Harinaam prabhu will cleanse your mind of materialistic inclination and purify your mind.

The Unparalleled Supremacy of the Hare Krishna Mahamantra

The Hare Krishna Mahamantra is called the Maha-Mantra, the king of all mantras, because it contains all divine potencies. It is not just a naam (holy name); it is a mantra. A mantra has specific, invested powers (shakti). Some mantras bestow knowledge, some bestow power. The Harinam Mahamantra is a *prema-prakashak* ‚Äî it is the bestower of Prema, or pure, divine love for the divine couple.

Hare Krishna, Hare Krishna, Krishna Krishna, Hare Hare
Hare Rama, Hare Rama, Rama Rama, Hare Hare
  • Hare: This refers to Radha Rani, who has the power to steal (haran) even the mind of Krishna.
  • Krishna: The all-attractive Supreme Lord.
  • Rama: This is not Dasharatha's son, Lord Rama. Here, it means Ramayate iti Rama‚ÄîKrishna, the one who engages in divine loving pastimes (raman) with all beings as the indwelling Supersoul.

Mahaprabhu made this mantra simple. There are no rules of time, place, or purity. You can chant while rising, sitting, eating, or sleeping. He invested all divine potencies into it. This is the king of mantras, the Maha Mantra, because there is no mantra above it. The fruit of other mantras may be liberation, but the fruit of Harinam is Prema‚Äîpure, divine love for Radha and Krishna, which is the highest attainment.

Any seeker from any spiritual path (sampradaya) who takes shelter of the Harinam Mahamantra will very quickly achieve their own spiritual goal (ishta-siddhi), because the Name will bestow its grace upon them. Many great ‡§∏‡§ø‡§¶‡•ç‡§ß-‡§Æ‡§π‡§æ‡§§‡•ç‡§Æ‡§æs (perfected saints) have chanted the Harinam Mahamantra. My own Gurudev attained perfection after Radharani Herself appeared and bestowed grace upon him at Prem Sarovar, yet he continued to chant the Harinam Mahamantra. If you look at the last 500 years, you will see that 70-80% of the saints mentioned in the Bhaktamal (stories of devotees) achieved perfection through the Hare Krishna Mahamantra.

Guidance for those without a Guru: The Potency of Radha Naam

If someone has not been able to find his Guru yet, and has no affiliation with any Acharya parampara, therefore, is he not able to progress in sadhana and receive mercy of Krishna? The *Padmapuran* has answered that "among all the names of Bhagwan, Radha naam is the superior most name". By chanting Radha naam, all sins wash away and Krishna himself tries to please and shower mercy on the sadhak. For a fallen soul victimized by the negative traits of Kaliyuga, the best path is to take shelter of Srimati Radharani and Radha naam, and pray to her:

"He Radharani, He Karunamayi, I take the shelter of your lotus feet. I am yours. Please save me! Please have mercy on me! Please protect me!"

To chant the Radha naam, there is no need for a guru, place, time, purity, or impurity. The one who has more attachment to Radha naam than Shri Krishna's name, that person is called a Radhanishtha devotee. By the grace of Radha naam, one day the sadhak will attain a guru, and then by guru's grace, they will attain the loving service of the divine couple Shri Radha Krishna.

For those who have found their Gurus, following their respective paths prescribed by their Gurus is the best path for them.

Harinam as the Highest Sadhana

"Sadhan madhye shreshtha navavidha bhakti, Krishna prem Krishna dite dhare mahashakti. Taar madhye sarvashreshtha naam sankirtan, niraparadhe layile naam paaye premodhan."

Meaning: "Among all forms of sadhanas, nine are best which are able to give Krishna Prem and consequently, Krishna himself. Among those nine, Harinaam sankirtan is the king of all sadhanas. If one avoids any kind of aparadhas, Harinaam prabhu gives the wealth of love of Krishna."

"True devotion is when the Lord's name is on your lips and His thought is in your mind with every moment". But our mind is entangled in the worldly realm, wondering when my chanting will be over, when I can finish my rounds and go outside. This is not genuine devotion.

The Three Modes of Japa: A Practical Guide

Harinam can be chanted (japa) in three ways: Vagyas japa, Manas japa and Upanshu japa. Each mode is suited for different environments, times of day, and levels of concentration, allowing the practitioner to remain absorbed in chanting under any circumstance.

Vagyas Japa (Loud Chanting)

This is Harinaam sankirtan or chanting loudly. In this form, Harinaam benefits both the speaker/singer and the listener. When there is a lot of commotion, for example if you are in the middle of a party, you cannot concentrate on harinaam without chanting it loudly. It is considered highly beneficial as it purifies not only the speaker but also any person or living entity who hears the sacred sound. This mode is ideal for congregational chanting (sankirtan), public chanting processions, or when one is in a private space where loud chanting is possible without causing disturbance. It is also useful for concentration in noisy environments.

Upanshu Japa (Whispered Chanting)

This mode involves moving the lips to form the syllables of the mantra, but so softly and quietly that only the chanter can hear it. This is practiced in a place where there is a lot of sound and one could neither do kirtan nor contemplate Harinaam, like in a marketplace. This method is exceptionally practical for chanting in public places, such as a marketplace, on public transport, or in any environment where loud chanting would be inappropriate or where maintaining mental focus amidst external noise is challenging.

Manas Japa (Mental Chanting)

This is chanting in one's mind, neither the lips will move nor there is any sound of Harinaam. Harinam will play like a recorded tape on loop inside the mind. But for this, we need total silence and a lot of concentration. The best time for Manas japa is when everyone is asleep at night and there is no sound anywhere. If someone asks whether we must contemplate on pastimes of Krishna while chanting, the answer is while doing Manas japa, one must concentrate on Harinaam only. If you contemplate on pastimes of Krishna, you will not focus on Harinaam and Harinaam will be given less importance. One must become engrossed in Harinaam while chanting and leave behind all other thoughts. This form of Harinaam sadhana is very powerful. But one must lessen one's need to sleep to sit in Padmasana and do Manas japa at night. One has to take satvik food and almost no dinner at night to stay awake. Or, one can sleep early and wake up at 1am to do Manas japa. (Baba says Shri Ram Vairagi Baba, who is a disciple of Shri Deoraha Baba and is 100 years old, still wakes up at 1pm at night daily and practices Manas japa. He sometimes comes over to Baba's ashram to stay for a few weeks.)

A sadhak should have full focus on Harinaam, it doesn't matter he is able to do any lila chintan or not. When Harinaam prabhu will shower mercy the pastimes of Krishna will emerge in the heart of sadhak on their own. Take the shelter of Harinaam, Naam ashray.

The Quality of Chanting: Shuddha Naam vs. Namabhas

The ultimate effectiveness and spiritual potency of chanting are determined not by the quantity of mantras recited, but by the quality of consciousness with which the chanting is performed. There is a profound distinction between pure chanting and its reflection.

Namabhas (The Reflection or Shadow of the Name)

This is a stage of chanting where the mind, despite one's best intentions, wanders to various worldly thoughts, anxieties, and desires‚Äîwork, family, future plans, past regrets‚Äîwhile the lips and tongue mechanically repeat the mantra simply to complete a prescribed number of rounds. If your mind is wandering to various thoughts, to Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, etc. and also chanting to complete the number of rounds you are supposed to finish then it is called Namabhas. It will not go futile but it will take a long time to achieve the fruits of chanting harinaam.

However, practicing Namabhas it slowly purifies one's heart and slowly the sadhak is able to beget the full mercy of harinaam. Then slowly he starts experiencing the pastimes of Radha Krishna as if he is present there. Slowly he is able to comprehend his actual form, that is the Manjari Swarupa. And serves Radha Krishna in that swarupa. So it is not that practicing Namabhas results in Prem. But practicing Namabhas slowly purifies one's heart so that the sadhak is able to chant Shuddha naam and then Shuddha naam gives Prem. First the sadhak enters and ascends through the stages of satsang, bhajan kriya, anartha nivritti, nishtha, ruchi, aasakti, bhav and lastly, Prem. It is not possible by the hardwork of sadhak to ascend through these stages, but it is harinaam that takes you through. You cannot deliberate yourself from the stage of ruchi to the stage of aasakti. It is possible only by the mercy of harinaam. Naam prabhu is not just a word, it is the same as naami, Krishna and holds all the powers of Krishna. That is why even Namabhas purifies one's heart and gives him the ability to chant Shuddha naam and gradually gives Prem. But yes, you have to beware of Naam aparadhas.

Shuddha Naam (Pure Chanting)

This is the stage of pure, unadulterated chanting, characterized by full and uninterrupted concentration on the holy name, completely free from all other extraneous thoughts (anya chinta rahit). That is, without any other thought. If we give our full concentration towards harinaam, we will get the fruits of chanting harinaam very quickly. At this stage, the holy name is not merely heard; it is realized as being non-different from the Supreme Lord Himself. When we chant harinaam along with chintan (contemplating the pastimes of Krishna and Radharani) it is called Shuddha naam. We have to think only of Radha Krishna and no material subject. Such kind of chanting provides the fruits of chanting harinaam very quickly. It is crucial to "chant Harinaam with utmost respect and love, and know that Naam prabhu is not different from Nami (Krishna)". The text emphasizes that "it is harinaam that takes you through" the stages of devotion, as "Naam prabhu is not just a word, it is the same as naami, Krishna and holds all the powers of Krishna".

Namaparadh (Offensive Chanting)

This is when a person is chanting while being offensive. This type of chanting will bear no result at all.

Contemplating the Guru while Chanting

For those who have found their Gurus, following their respective paths prescribed by their Gurus is the best path for them. Contemplating on the image of Guru, or the lotus feet of Guru, is "even more appreciated in our scriptures". Since Guru is not a human made up of flesh and blood, but the highest tatva who has been honored by even Ram and Krishna (by accepting Vashishtha rishi and Sandipani muni as Gurus, respectively), the attitude of resolute bhakti of Guru is said to be rare and more desirable than devotion to Krishna. "Kimparamadhikam Krishnebhaktih na guror adhikam na guror adhikam". But one must have firm faith that Guru is not a human being, Guru tatva is non different from Isvara tatva. While contemplating on the image of Guru, the image of Krishna will emerge from the image of Guru by the mercy of Gurudev. Indeed, one who has firm and unfailing faith in Guru is rare.

The Role of Surrender in Chanting

To be surrendered, does the mind have to be pure? There is no waiting for purity. The Lord accepts a devotee the moment he surrenders. In the Gita, the Lord says, "Even if a person of the most evil conduct worships Me with undivided devotion, he should be regarded as righteous, for he has rightly resolved." The Lord continues, "He quickly becomes a righteous soul and attains lasting peace. O son of Kunti, declare it boldly that My devotee never perishes!" If a person were already pure, there would be no need for the Lord to make such a bold promise. The promise is powerful precisely because the person was sinful, but by taking refuge, they are now protected. The moment a devotee, no matter how sinful, surrenders to the Lord, the Lord accepts him as His own. He doesn't wait for purity or good habits. Why? Because the Lord knows that the soul, on its own, does not have the power to cross over His divine energy, maya. He Himself says, "Mama maya duratyaya" (My divine energy is difficult to overcome). Therefore, there is no question of sin or virtue at the time of surrender. The only means of deliverance is the Lord's grace.

Sometimes, even after surrendering, maya can knock a devotee down. This is to make him stronger. It is a practical experience. The Lord makes him stumble to teach him a lesson. The devotee then realizes, "Oh, I made this mistake. I must never do it again." It's not that after a little bit of bhajan, you become free from all obstacles. The path is long. Have you ever been to the Himalayas? When you are ascending a mountain, sometimes the path goes down. An onlooker might say, "You are going down, not up!" But the path is such that after going down 1000 steps, you will have to climb 2000 steps to continue your ascent. It may look like a fall, but it is progress. You might see a devotee and think, "Oh, he has fallen, he is caught in maya," but you don't understand. He has to learn some experience there. The Lord is making him stronger.

Some karmic reactions are very strong (durbar bhog) and must be endured. But smaller ones can be mitigated by the grace of the Guru and God. Pray. Every night before sleeping, present whatever small desires you have in your heart to the Lord. Pray sincerely, "Please remove this, remove this from my heart." I have seen this in my own practical experience‚ÄîHe removes it. He erases it. Pray with tears, with desperation: "Radharani, this wicked desire in my heart is a terrible obstacle to attaining Your lotus feet. Protect me, save me, remove it from within me!" It is not that these things cannot be erased. But the prayer must be from the heart, not just with words. He does not listen to what your lips are saying; He listens to your heart.

The Esoteric Mood of a Practitioner: Radha Within, Krishna Without

According to the esoteric teachings of the Gaudiya Vaishnava tradition, the internal mood of a practitioner is beautifully encapsulated in the phrase "Radha within, Krishna without." The external chanting of Krishna's name is performed with the primary intention of bringing pleasure to Srimati Radharani, who becomes overwhelmed with ecstatic joy upon hearing the name of Her beloved. This is the profound and confidential secret of the practice, where the ultimate goal is to please the divine pleasure potency of the Lord.

People don't know our path of worship (upasana). We are very dear to Radharani, but others don't understand our method. Our worship is that of a Radha-snehika‚Äîone who is affectionate to Radha, like Her closest handmaidens (manjaris).

There are five types of sakhis (friends of Radha): sakhi, priya-sakhi, priya-narma-sakhi, prana-sakhi, and nitya-sakhi. The manjaris are nitya-sakhis. They are always with Radharani. Her happiness is their life, their everything, their all-in-all. Their senses are dedicated to Her pleasure.

This spiritual form of a manjari is bestowed only by Radharani's grace. It cannot be achieved through one's own efforts or austerities. Only those upon whom Radharani bestows this mercy can attain that form. A manjari is like a spark (sphuran) from Radharani's own being. She is a miniature, unmanifested form of Radha. The only difference is the physical body (deho bheda matra); in essence, she is non-different from Radharani. She knows nothing but Radha.

Mor praneshwari Radha, Vrindavaneshwari, taar prananath boli bhoji Giridhari.
(My life's queen is Radha, the Empress of Vrindavan. Knowing that Krishna is the Lord of Her life, I worship Him.)

So, we worship Krishna because He is the Lord of Radharani's life. When Krishna is in an angry mood (maan), we do not let him enter the divine bower (kunja). Our service is to Radharani. That is why our internal reality is "Radha within, Krishna without." We chant "Hare Krishna, Hare Krishna" externally, but internally, our hearts are chanting "Radha, Radha, Radha." We know nothing else.

The speaker explains the inner dynamic of pleasing Radha and Krishna: If you go directly to Radharani and chant, "Radhe, Radhe, Radhe," She does not feel as much joy and happiness. But if you go to Krishna and whisper "Radhe, Radhe" in His ear, He becomes completely ecstatic and thinks, "Who is this person who has made me hear Radha's name?" Similarly, if you go to Radharani and chant, "Krishna, Krishna, Krishna," She becomes overwhelmed with ecstatic love (pulokangi) and a great ocean of bliss erupts within Her. This is why we chant Krishna's name‚Äîto please Radharani.

Overcoming Obstacles and Measuring True Progress

Cultivating Steadfastness (Nishtha)

It is of paramount importance to have unwavering faith and steadfastness (nishtha) in one mantra. If you chant many different mantras, your nishtha is harmed. If you are a devotee of Sita-Ram, then stay with that name. Once you have established your path, you must not waver. If you keep switching‚Äîsometimes this, sometimes that‚Äîyour mind will never become fixed. You cannot make progress. You must have firm faith in the name you have been given. Only then will the chanting become effortless and automatic, arising from a deep inner conviction.

Dealing with Setbacks and the Ebb and Flow of Bliss

The experience of bliss and spiritual connection in chanting may often seem to come and go. The answer is our continued association with the material world (vishaya). How can darkness and light exist in the same place? If there is light, darkness must go. Similarly, if you are attached to worldly sense objects and pleasures, how can you expect to constantly taste the bliss of the transcendental world? For the experience to be constant, one needs solitude and detachment from material entanglement. This is primarily attributed to our continued association with worldly sense objects and the pursuit of material pleasures (vishaya). Just as light and darkness cannot coexist in the same space, the transcendental bliss of Harinaam cannot be constantly and fully experienced while the heart remains attached to the material world. Therefore, a sincere practitioner must be ever-cautious of worldly attachments, which are described as "looters" that relentlessly pull the soul back into the painful cycle of birth, death, old age, and disease. For the experience to be constant, one needs solitude and detachment from material entanglement.

The True Signs of Spiritual Progress

What are the symptoms of a purified mind? By repeated chanting, the mind is purified and softened, and the sadhak sheds tears, shivers and gets goosebumps while chanting Harinaam.

Physical Manifestations of Ecstasy

"Vak gadagadaya dravatachetsapina,
vina anandasrukalaya katham siddhir vinashaya."

Meaning: By repeated chanting, listening to satsanga and other bhakti yogas, if the throat is not obstructed due to overwhelming emotions, the chitta or mind is not softened, the eyes are not brimming with tears of joy (there is divine ecstasy in the contemplation of God and with prolonged chintan the sadhak gets a taste of it), then how do we know the mind of a sadhak has been purified? (Without the symptoms of asru (tears), kampa (shivering) and pulak (goosebumps), the sadhak cannot be said to have achieved a pure mind, let alone acquire bhakti).

Internal Purity and Humility

A key and unmistakable sign of progress is when you begin to genuinely see the good qualities in all other living beings and to recognize the faults and shortcomings within yourself. The cultivation of true humility is a hallmark of an advancing devotee. The mind contains conditioning from countless lifetimes. "When the sun of satsang and bhajan rises, this conditioning is obscured." However, if that sun sets, the darkness and negative qualities return. To dispel this darkness, one must "light the lamp. Live in solitude, purify your diet, stay away from worldly people and worldly talk."

Detachment and Equanimity

A progressively decreasing desire for worldly pleasures and the ability to remain steady, peaceful, and undisturbed in the face of both happiness and distress are clear marks of a true devotee who is transcending the dualities of the material world. One who is equal in happiness and distress. For whom a stone and gold are the same. Not that gold is valuable and a stone is just a stone‚Äîno, both are equal. One for whom friend and foe are the same. Praise and insult are the same. One who remains steady in honor and dishonor. For whom my dear ones and my enemies are both equal. Such a person has transcended the three modes of material nature. You can't just take one and leave the other. If you take one, you must take its opposite. Therefore, it is said, take nothing‚Äîneither happiness nor distress, neither honor nor dishonor. The story of Shrivas Pandit, who danced and sang during *kirtan* despite the death of his son, illustrates the supreme importance given to chanting and the devotee's detachment.

Vinod Baba's Personal Testimony: A Beacon of Hope

When I left home to do bhajan, I had no idea about initiation or a guru. I just knew I had to leave. For five years, I lived in solitude. After studying many scriptures, I concluded three things are essential: solitude (ekantata), chanting the holy name (Harinam Sankirtan), and the company of saints (sadhu-sanga). I had tried every path‚ÄîTantra, Yoga, Jnana‚Äîbut all scriptures concluded that in this age, Harinam is the only way.

So I started chanting. I didn't know anything else. I would lock my door day and night and just chant Harinam. I can't describe the bliss I felt. Sometimes it felt as if I had drunk liquor. I would open my eyes and see nothing; I was in a state of complete intoxication. I had no initiation, no guru, but Radha Rani herself showed me many things practically. That is why I insist with so much force: whether you like it or not, do not leave the holy name. This is my practical experience, not something I just heard.

‚Äî Sri Sri Vinod Bihari Das Baba Ji Maharaj

This extraordinary and deeply personal account profoundly underscores the inherent, independent potency of the Holy Name. It serves as a powerful testament to the fact that the Holy Name itself is fully capable of acting as the Guru, revealing all spiritual knowledge and leading the sincere seeker to the highest and most sublime states of divine love.

The Transformative Power of Harinam: The Story of Hari Baba

The life of Hari Baba serves as a powerful testament to the transformative power of Harinam. From birth, Hari Baba was exceptionally intelligent. His father enrolled him in school, which he completed quickly. He was then sent to Lahore for medical studies, but his mind wasn't in it; his mind was set on bhajan. He understood that even if one gains wealth, fame, and all kinds of pleasures in this human body, life is wasted if one doesn't attain devotion to Hari. He had been deeply influenced by a great saint who taught him, "This body is perishable, this world is perishable. God has mercifully given you this human form, and through this body, attaining God is the only possibility." This thought completely detached his mind from the worldly cycle.

His guru was Satchidananda Giri Maharaj, who was a follower of the formless Absolute (Nirguna Brahman). Hari Baba went to his guru's feet and said, "Baba, I don't like these studies. Please have mercy. Give me sannyasa (the vows of renunciation)." The guru explained, "True renunciation happens in the mind. A true sannyasi is one who is free from both desire and aversion, who has transcended all dualities. For such a person, wearing the ochre cloth is not necessary. When the mind detaches from all worldly objects and becomes singularly focused on the Lord's feet, that is true renunciation. Just as a fully ripened fruit falls from the tree on its own, when a seeker's inner dispassion (vairagya) becomes intense, the world of desire‚Äîfamily, home, business‚Äînaturally falls away. The world is not the house or shop; the world is desire (vasana). If you have desires for sense enjoyment, you are worldly. If you have the desire for God, you are a renunciate. It's that simple."

Despite this counsel, Hari Baba left everything and went straight to Kashi (Varanasi). There, he took up the ochre robes of a renunciate himself. His austerities were intense. He would beg for his food (madhukari), and if he received more than two or four pieces of bread, he would wrap the excess in a cloth, bury it in the sand, and eat it the next day after washing it. In Kashi, he came into the company of other great saints like Oriya Baba and Premananda Baba, and his mind became intensely absorbed in bhajan. Yet, he felt something was missing. A seeker often grows disheartened, feeling that despite all efforts, the goal is not being reached. But one must never lose hope, for the Lord is all-merciful.

Hari Baba was a follower of the formless Brahman. He studied Vedanta in Wardha. The followers of this path do not believe in a personal God with form or qualities; they believe in an attributeless, unchanging, and unmanifest Absolute. All that we see is considered maya (illusion).

One day, a saint gave him a book titled Lord Gauranga, written by the great Bengali devotee Shishir Kumar Ghosh. After reading this book, his entire perspective shifted. His mind turned away from the worship of the formless Brahman and became completely attracted to the Saguna Savishesh‚Äîthe personal God with a divine name, form, and transcendental qualities like compassion and kindness, which are not of this material world.

"Atmaramascha munayo nirgrantha apy urukrame, kurvanti ahaitukim bhaktim ittham-bhuta-guno harih."

Meaning: Even the self-satisfied sages, who are freed from the knot of matter and spirit, are irresistibly attracted to the transcendental qualities of Lord Hari and engage in His selfless, causeless devotion. This verse settled deep into his mind. The "knot" is the identification of the soul (consciousness) with the body (matter). Liberation from this knot is the ultimate goal of the path of knowledge. Yet, this verse says that even such liberated souls are attracted to the qualities of Lord Krishna and perform His devotion. After this, Hari Baba gave up the path of formless worship and became completely absorbed in Harinam Sankirtan (the congregational chanting of the holy names) in the mood of Chaitanya Mahaprabhu.

Ecstasy and Spreading the Holy Name

He began to chant "Hare Krishna" wherever he went. Sometimes, he would be seen meditating, and at other times, he would be dancing and crying in ecstasy. Those on the path of knowledge couldn't understand. They would say, "Why does the soul cry? The soul is pure bliss." But these were not tears of worldly sorrow. When you unexpectedly meet a loved one after a long time, you cry out of overwhelming joy. These were tears of bliss (ananda-ashru), tears of divine love.

Hari Baba became mad with love for the holy name and went to extraordinary lengths to make others chant:

  • He carried a pot-seller's heavy load for three kilometers, asking only that the man promise to chant the Hare Krishna Mahamantra in return.
  • He joined a farmer cutting grass and offered to help, asking him to chant the holy name.
  • He played kabaddi with children, telling them to chant "Hare Krishna, Hare Krishna!" instead of "kabaddi, kabaddi".
  • Through the power of continuous Harinam kirtan, he revived a dying boy and inspired thousands to build an enormous, 40-kilometer-long dam to prevent floods.

Additional Teachings

The Danger of Worldly Affection

"How terrifying this is! This worldly "love" ensnares us and pulls us away from the lotus feet of Radharani, dragging us back towards the material world (samsara). We don't even realize what a terrible thing we are doing by engaging in this "love." We think, "Oh, these loving people are our well-wishers." But these very people, wrapping us in their affection, are leading us back into the world.

We have attained this human body, which means we have reached the shore of the ocean of birth and death. This human body is a boat given to us to cross this ocean. But our so-called "loved ones," in whom we placed our trust, have ensnared us in affection and are pulling us back. We have no idea. We have become fools, seeing what is harmful as beneficial (anartha artha-darshina), and we place our trust in that which is dangerous.

As the saying goes:
"Humko gairon ne nahi, yaaron ne loota."
(It was not strangers who looted me, but my own dear ones.)
Strangers wouldn't have had the power to do it. The one who is my enemy could not loot me. But my "friends," those I love, have looted me in such a way that I didn't even realize I was being robbed. I trusted them completely, and they kept looting the treasure of my spiritual progress. The grief I feel is this: if I had drowned in the middle of the deep ocean, I would n't have been so sad. My greatest sorrow is that I came all the way to the shore and drowned just a few steps away. This human body is a boat. You have been given a chance to cross the ocean of material existence. You have almost reached the shore, but right at the edge, you are made to drown. This is the greatest tragedy.

One must live in fear‚Äînot fear of the world, but fear of being looted. Every moment, you must be cautious that no one pulls you back into this ocean of suffering. You have been given this one chance, this human body, to cross over. Your enemy is better for you; he criticizes you, defames you, and that makes a spiritual seeker vigilant. "Oh, people are slandering me, I must be careful so that no one can say such things." But your "loved ones"? They will lift you up only to slam you down. You must be saved from these dear ones. How will you be saved? That is for you and your karma to decide.

There is one way to be saved: first, recognize them. When someone shows you affection, be wary. The most dangerous thing is if you love someone. If someone loves you, it is less dangerous; you might be able to escape. But if you start loving them, then you will surely be looted. Therefore, you must be careful not to love anyone in this way. See everyone as a form of God, but do not get attached to a particular person‚Äîbe it a wife, a son, a daughter, or anyone else where the mind gets stuck. This is dangerous.

When you plant a sapling, you must protect it with a fence of thorns, because goats and cows will come and eat it. But when the tree grows large, you can tie an elephant to it, and nothing will happen. Similarly, in the beginning of your spiritual life, you must be saved from "dear ones." Don't love anyone in this way. If you do, know for certain that your boat will be sunk right at the shore by these very people.

The Nature of Grace and Surrender

It is not an easy path of just eating and sleeping well and then going to the spiritual world. You have to endure hardships. Even great beings like Brahma are under the influence of maya; no one crosses it without the grace of God. True surrender (sharanagati) is the key.

The grace of the Guru is always flowing, like the wind. It is not that there is more grace for one person and less for another. The difference is in your capacity to receive it (patrata). How much have you accepted?

Consider this analogy: A father is walking with his two sons. One son is very small and can't walk, so the father carries him in his arms. The other son is walking alongside, holding his father's hand. A helicopter flies overhead. The son who was holding his father's hand gets excited, points up, "Look, Papa, look!" and, distracted, he stumbles on a stone and falls, getting badly hurt. The little child being carried in his father's arms also gets excited and points, "Papa, Papa!" but he does not fall. He is not hurt. Why? Are they not both the father's sons? Does the father love one more and the other less? No. The difference is that one son was walking by holding onto the father, while the other son was being held by the father. Because the father was holding him, there was no danger of him falling. The other son was relying on his own effort to hold on, and so he fell.

This is the nature of surrender. One type of devotee is completely surrendered in every situation, trusting, "You do as You wish." The Lord Himself takes hold of such a devotee. The other type relies on his own efforts: "I have to do this chanting, this austerity, this and that." People like this often boast, "I am a devotee; what can maya do to me?" One who walks independently like this will fall.
